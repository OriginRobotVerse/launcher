01 — Essence

Brand Character

Origin is the mathematical zero point — (0,0) — the fixed reference from which everything is measured. The precise coordinate where firmware stops being code you fight with and becomes infrastructure you build on.

Precise
A coordinate system. Exact, reliable, zero ambiguity. The firmware loop is a single call: origin.tick()
Bridging
Two worlds connected — the physical (Arduino, sensors, motors) and the logical (TypeScript, ML, application code).
Foundational
The base layer. Flash once, build forever. All logic changes happen on the host — the firmware is fixed infrastructure.
Minimal
Stays out of the way. Register your hardware, define your actions, and Origin handles the rest. No boilerplate, no ceremony.
Tagline
Origin — The zero point between hardware and code.
02 — Logo

The Origin Point

A crosshair centered on a coordinate origin. Two perpendicular axes intersect with a solid square at center — the (0,0) point. Sharp, geometric, zero border-radius. The lines break around the center point so it floats at the intersection.

Mark Only
Favicons, app icons, small contexts
Wordmark

Full Lockup
Landing pages, presentations, hero sections

Construction

Center Point
Solid square. Never a circle. Zero border-radius. Size = 1 unit on the grid.
Axes
Two perpendicular lines of equal length, extending from the gap edge outward. Stroke weight is consistent.
Gap
Lines break around the center square. Gap width = center square width. The point floats at the intersection.
Tick Marks
Optional. Small perpendicular ticks at equal intervals along the axes. Used in detailed variant only. Reduced opacity.
Proportion
Axis length from gap to terminal = 3.5× the center square width. Total mark fits a square bounding box.

Rules

◼
One accent, sparingly
Phosphor amber on interactive elements, the origin point, active states, and key data. Never as large background fills.
◼
Dark backgrounds only
Origin never uses a light theme. The void is the canvas. All content sits on #0A0A0A or elevated surfaces.
◼
No gradients
Flat color only. Gradients dilute precision. Depth comes from surface elevation, not blending.
◼
Structural borders
Wire (#1E1E1E) delineates panels. It's architecture, not ornament. Borders define space, they don't decorate it.

05 — Iconography

The Bot

Origin's mascot is a small boxy robot with binocular eyes on stalks, always looking up — the device gazing at the host, waiting for its next instruction. Inspired by the original WALL·E Arduino project that started it all. Sharp geometry, zero border-radius, stroke-only construction.

Eyes
Binocular housings on stalks. Always looking up. Pupils are phosphor amber squares — the only accent in the character. They sit at the top of each eye housing to convey the upward gaze.
Body
Rectangular chassis. Visible panel lines and detail geometry. One small amber indicator light on the body. All rectangles, zero border-radius.
Treads
Two track units with interior tread lines. Grounded and mechanical. They establish the bot as something that moves in the physical world.
Arms
Simple angled lines terminating in small rectangular grippers. Resting position is slightly lowered — not waving, not posed. Neutral and ready.
The Gaze
The bot always looks up. It's the device looking at the host for its next action. This upward orientation is the single most important quality of the character and must be preserved in every variant.

06 — Usage

Do & Don't

Consistency is precision. These rules keep Origin looking like Origin.

✓ DO
Use the mark on dark backgrounds (#0A0A0A or #111111)
Keep clear space of at least 1× the center square width
Use phosphor amber as a single restrained accent
Render the center point as a square — always
Use JetBrains Mono exclusively across all materials
Use flat color — surface depth via elevation only
✗ DON'T
Place the mark on light or white backgrounds
Use rounded corners on the center point or any element
Apply gradients, shadows, or glows to the mark
Rotate, skew, or distort the crosshair
Mix in a second typeface alongside JetBrains Mono
Use amber as a large fill — it's an accent, not a surface
Separate the crosshair from the center point
